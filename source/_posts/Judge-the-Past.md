# Where is the sense? Why the leader of MMYZ always wants to ban the takeway food HYSTERICALLY?

For MMYZ, <font color="yellow">**the most dramatic part of its story is the long-running “battle” with students over ordering takeaway food**</font>. Everyone knows that when MMYZ was moved out to a place in the middle of nowhere, the biggest headache was the lack of food variety, not to mention that the school has never really cared much about improving the quality of the canteen meals. Because of this, for students at MMYZ, there has been always a simple but important problem: how to actually eat better at school.

2013, MMYZ relocated to the Gaodi area. In theory, food-related problems should have emerged at this time. However, in reality, the contradiction between students’ demand for food variety and the school relocation was not very sharp at the beginning.In 2020, the school snack shop was suddenly closed quietly without any clear reason. Earlier than that, there was a syrup shop next to the police office in the east zone. Unfortunately, for most students, they could only see its remaining signboard. Later, it was turned into a base for the Communist Youth League (CYL).In 2022, the school grocery store was also forced to close due to conflicts of interest between the former principal—who has been imprisoned—and the new leadership team. If Media Real Estate (aka “Media RE” ) chooses to build high-profit buildings in other wastelands instead, many sugar lovers might go through three years of high school without drinking iced tea many times.

I just gave a few examples, but they are enough to show that before 2015 or even earlier, students of MMYZ at No. 8 Haixin Road were living a relatively happy school life. It can be imagined that, in its early stage, MMYZ tried to create such a wide range of conditions for students partly as a message to parents: to prove that even after being moved to the suburban area of Dianbai, students’ daily life could still be as good as what they used to have in the city center.

However, in 2015, Maoming Polytechnic (M.P.) set up its South Campus just one street away from MMYZ. Many nearby farmers quickly spotted a business opportunity and set up small stalls right in front of the M.P. gate. The local government also saw this as a chance to boost economic growth. As a result, under the promotion of the district government, a food street was built at the entrance of M.P. A few years later, barber shops, snack stores, printing shops, and other businesses also appeared around this area. While the surroundings of MMYZ were still mostly weeds and rural land, a complete commercial ecosystem had already formed in front of M.P. However, those farmers who were good at doing business would not limit their target customers only to students at M.P. Gradually, for the leadership of MMYZ, the “barrier” they once imagined began to slowly fall apart.
